30. **Stale-mail rule:** periodically list stale recruiting threads by age and recommended disposition. Do not delete, auto-archive, or remove messages solely because they are stale; organizational changes require explicit authorization.

- Flexible, truthful career narrative: either 20+ years of broad technical/industry depth or five years of recent AI/ML systems work, selected according to role seniority and application tone

### 2.2 Execution risks

- Broad generalist experience can obscure the strongest role-specific story
- Depth is not uniform across every framework listed on the resume
- Some interviews require stronger concise communication and more structured behavioral answers
- Highly specialized research roles may expect direct publication or implementation evidence in a narrow subfield
- Fast or unfamiliar accents may require deliberate clarification during live calls
- Career-stage bias may exist in some recruiting funnels
- Education dates, excessive early-career chronology, or unnecessary date disclosures can enable age inference
- An overly long answer can reduce perceived clarity even when the technical content is strong

### 2.3 Mitigation rules

- Tailor the first 60 seconds to the exact role
- Prepare three to five role-specific stories before each interview
- Use: **problem → action → measurable result → relevance**
- State direct experience separately from adjacent or transferable experience
- Ask interviewers to repeat or rephrase unclear questions without apology
- Prefer senior, principal, staff, founding, platform, and systems roles where breadth is an advantage
- Omit all education dates from outward-facing recruiting materials; provide them only when mandatory in a verified background-check or onboarding process
- Choose either the 20+ years broad-experience frame or the five-year recent-AI frame before drafting; do not mix both by default
- Do not list a framework unless it can be explained fluently enough for a technical screen
- Keep recruiter replies concise; move detailed technical evidence to the resume or interview

## 4. Opportunity Scoring

Use a 100-point score for serious opportunities.

| Dimension | Weight | Guidance |
|---|---:|---|
| Technical fit | 30 | Agentic AI, AI systems, Python, platform, infrastructure, evaluation |
| Compensation | 20 | Compare with the target bands below |
| Work arrangement | 15 | Fully remote highest; Bay Area hybrid next |
| Seniority and ownership | 10 | Staff, principal, lead, founding, architecture ownership |
| Employer / client quality | 10 | Direct employer, credible startup, named end client, strong funding |
| Recruiter/process quality | 10 | Clear JD, rate, client, timeline, direct contact, responsiveness |
| Strategic value | 5 | Brand, network, conversion, research, consulting, or referral value |

### 4.1 Priority bands

- **85–100:** Immediate action
- **70–84:** High priority
- **55–69:** Conditional / qualify first
- **40–54:** Backup / low priority
- **Below 40:** Decline or skip

### 4.2 Override rules

A role may be elevated despite imperfect fit when it has:

- $300K+ base or exceptional total compensation
- Strong founder equity and credible funding
- Direct access to a high-value hiring manager
- A realistic path into Staff / Principal AI systems work
- A uniquely strong brand, research environment, or network effect

- Long autobiographical messages instead of a role-specific value proposition.
- Excessive links, large unsolicited attachments, or sensitive documents.
- Multiple submissions to the same requisition through different agencies.
