## 0. Non-Negotiable Operating Rules

1. **Do not do C2C contracts.**
2. Prefer **direct full-time employment**, **W-2 full-time**, or **W-2 contract**.
3. Prioritize **fully remote roles first**, then strong Bay Area hybrid roles, then exceptional Bay Area onsite roles.
4. Maintain the identity **Agentic AI Engineer | AI Systems Engineer** across resumes, email, LinkedIn, portals, and interviews.
5. Use **“I am available now”**. Do not use “I am available immediately.”
6. Use the exact work-authorization wording: **“Work authorization: U.S. citizen; no sponsorship required.”**
7. Do not send sensitive identity, tax, immigration, background-check, or onboarding documents during ordinary applications.
8. When a recruiter asks for a resume, CV, PDF, updated resume, or attachment, send the **actual PDF**. An inline summary does not count.
9. Do not claim that an email was sent unless it appears in **Sent** or delivery is otherwise verified.
10. Do not count internal forwards, messages to Amy, duplicate copies, or recruiter-only routing messages as separate applications.
11. Follow up after approximately **7 calendar days**, unless the recruiter asks for something sooner.
12. Respond the same day to interview requests, screening questions, call requests, scheduling links, and document requests.
13. Do not argue with rejections. One calm clarification is allowed; then stop.
14. High-value old chains may be revived when the company, recruiter relationship, compensation, or strategic value is strong.
15. Decline financial-planning, wealth-management, tax, retirement, real-estate, insurance, webinar, newsletter, and unrelated sales outreach.
16. LinkedIn messages, attachments, portal submissions, calendar bookings, and phone calls are manual unless a connected tool explicitly supports the action and the result is verified.
17. Periodic campaign review means reviewing all sources during an active mission session. It does **not** imply autonomous background monitoring.
18. Every substantial Gmail review must cover both Gmail accounts, the parent `Jobs` label, **all current and future `Jobs/...` sublabels**, and relevant Sent mail.
19. Do not send references before an interview or confirmed client interest unless there is a compelling and verified reason.
20. Never use unsupported experience claims. Translate adjacent experience honestly instead of overstating direct expertise.
21. For a highest-value strategic contact, send one consolidated, decision-useful message and then wait. Online status, profile activity, or anxiety is not a follow-up trigger.
22. Follow the priority order in the current dated snapshot. Do not let lower-priority outreach delay a scheduled interview, client call, or high-value introduction.
23. When a recruiter or talent agent is arranging an introduction, do not apply directly to the same company or requisition until representation and submission status are clear.
24. If an account, portal, label, attachment, or source cannot be accessed, state that limitation explicitly. Never describe a partial review as a complete audit.

- Strong transferability across Azure, AWS, GCP, Kubernetes, MLflow, Spark, Databricks, SQL, and accelerator platforms

### 2.2 Execution risks

- Broad generalist experience can obscure the strongest role-specific story
- Depth is not uniform across every framework listed on the resume
- Some interviews require stronger concise communication and more structured behavioral answers
- Highly specialized research roles may expect direct publication or implementation evidence in a narrow subfield
- Fast or unfamiliar accents may require deliberate clarification during live calls
- Career-stage bias may exist in some recruiting funnels
- An overly long answer can reduce perceived clarity even when the technical content is strong

### 2.3 Mitigation rules

- Tailor the first 60 seconds to the exact role
- Prepare three to five role-specific stories before each interview
- Use: **problem → action → measurable result → relevance**
- State direct experience separately from adjacent or transferable experience
- Ask interviewers to repeat or rephrase unclear questions without apology
- Prefer senior, principal, staff, founding, platform, and systems roles where breadth is an advantage
- Do not list a framework unless it can be explained fluently enough for a technical screen
- Keep recruiter replies concise; move detailed technical evidence to the resume or interview

- $300K+ base or exceptional total compensation

- Direct access to a high-value hiring manager

## 11. Gmail Campaign Rules

### 11.1 What counts as an application

Count only a message or portal action directed to:

- A recruiter handling a specific role
- A hiring manager
- A company domain
- A direct client
- An application system
- A staffing contact handling a defined requisition

Do not count:

- Messages to Amy
- Internal forwards
- Duplicate copies
- “FYI” messages
- Pure networking without a role
- Recruiter-routing messages before a live requisition is confirmed

### 11.2 Two-account scope and audit disclosure

Attempt to review both:

```text
artemponomarevjetski@gmail.com
art.ponomarev.ai@gmail.com
```

If only one mailbox is connected or searchable:

- Audit the accessible mailbox completely.
- State exactly which mailbox was reviewed.
- State which mailbox was not independently accessible.
- Do not claim a complete two-account review.
- Treat copied or forwarded messages from the inaccessible mailbox as evidence only for those specific threads.

### 11.3 Jobs-label hierarchy

Every substantial review must independently inspect:

```text
Jobs
Jobs/Remote Priority
Jobs/Call Priority
Jobs/USTech
Jobs/SEAQR
Jobs/Sensitive Docs Review
```

Also discover and include every future label whose name starts with `Jobs/`.

Gmail nesting does not guarantee that a child-labeled message also has the parent `Jobs` label. Query the parent and each child independently.

### 11.4 Search both Inbox and Sent

- Inspect new inbound recruiter messages.
- Inspect Sent before claiming an action was completed.
- Search for delivery failures and bounced addresses.
- Search for duplicate recruiter submissions.
- Check whether an actual attachment was included.

### 11.5 Connected-capability boundary

Use only actions supported by the connected Gmail interface.

- Search and read threads before replying.
- Use drafts when review is appropriate.
- Send only when the user explicitly requests immediate sending or the mission instruction clearly authorizes it.
- Verify sent state after any send action.
- Verify labels, archive, Trash, forwarding, or draft updates after any write action.
- Attachments may require manual handling when the connected tool does not support outbound attachment creation.

When an action is unsupported, provide the exact manual procedure and do not claim completion.

### 11.6 Old-chain rule

Revive an old chain when:

- The recruiter relationship is real
- The company is high value
- Compensation is strong
- The prior process was credible
- Re-entry eligibility has passed
- Continuity improves the chance of response

Use a new standalone message for a clearly different role when an old subject line would create confusion.

### 11.7 No unverified sent claims

A message is not “sent” until:

- It appears in Sent, or
- A supported sending tool returns confirmed delivery state

A draft is a draft. A failed payload is not an email. A bounced message is not a delivered application.
